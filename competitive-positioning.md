# Katana Competitive Positioning

_Researched March 2026_

---

## Market Landscape

The AI grading tool market falls into three camps, all with meaningful friction:

1. **Copy-paste tools** (Magic School AI, Writable) — zero LMS awareness; teachers manually copy submissions in, copy feedback out
2. **Separate apps with grade sync** (EssayGrader, CoGrader, GradeWithAI) — require switching to another platform, doing the grading there, then syncing back
3. **LTI tools embedded in Canvas** (Turnitin, Gradescope, LearnWise, Khanmigo) — closest to native but require an IT administrator to install them at the institution level; individual teachers can't adopt them independently

**Katana's only real extension competitor is VibeGrade** (Y Combinator-backed). They work inside Canvas but appear to produce annotation overlays, not native rubric field auto-fill. Worth keeping an eye on — install their extension and compare actual rubric-filling behavior to Katana's.

### "How it's different" Section

**Works where you already work**
Most AI grading tools send you to a separate app — import submissions, grade there, export back. Katana works inside Canvas SpeedGrader. Open a student's submission, click Grade, and Katana fills in the rubric scores, grade, and feedback automatically. Review it, adjust if needed, submit. Done.

**Zero IT involvement**
Tools like Turnitin, Gradescope, and Grammarly for Education require your institution's IT department to install and configure them — a process that can take months and may never happen. Katana is a Chrome extension you install yourself in 60 seconds. No tickets, no approvals, no waiting.

**No copy-pasting. Ever.**
Other tools ask you to copy the student's submission, paste it into their platform, then copy the AI feedback back into Canvas. Katana reads the submission directly and writes feedback directly — all without you touching the clipboard.
